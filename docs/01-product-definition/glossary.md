Glossary

Introduction

This glossary defines the core business terms used throughout Priorities Tracker documentation.

The objective is to establish a consistent ubiquitous language across Product, Domain, Database, API, and Implementation phases.

⸻

Core Concepts

Commitment

A declared intention to complete specific work within a planning cycle.

Commitments are represented by priorities and supporting tasks.

⸻

Priority

A high-level work item that represents a commitment for a planning cycle.

Priorities are the primary planning objects within the platform.

⸻

Task

A specific activity associated with a priority.

Tasks provide additional execution detail but do not replace priorities.

⸻

Planning Cycle

A recurring period used for planning and reporting commitments.

The default planning cycle is one week.

⸻

Weekly Check-In

The process through which an employee records commitments for an upcoming planning cycle.

⸻

Weekly Check-Out

The process through which an employee records results achieved during a planning cycle.

⸻

Organizational Concepts

Employee

A platform user responsible for creating and executing commitments.

⸻

Manager

A platform user responsible for reviewing commitments, progress, risks, and execution reliability.

⸻

Administrator

A platform user responsible for platform configuration and organizational management.

⸻

Team

A group of employees managed by one or more managers.

⸻

Project Concepts

Project

A business initiative used to categorize priorities.

Projects provide context and reporting dimensions.

⸻

Project Phase

A subdivision of a project representing a stage of work.

Examples:

* Discovery
* Design
* Development
* Testing
* Deployment

⸻

Execution Concepts

Progress Update

A status update recorded during a planning cycle.

⸻

Blocker

A condition that prevents or delays progress.

Examples:

* External dependency
* Missing information
* Technical issue
* Pending approval

⸻

Risk

A potential future issue that may negatively affect commitment completion.

⸻

Carry-Over

A priority or task that is moved to a future planning cycle because it was not completed.

⸻

Reporting Concepts

Dashboard

A visual representation of commitments, progress, risks, and reliability metrics.

⸻

Team Dashboard

A dashboard focused on team-level visibility.

⸻

Employee Dashboard

A dashboard focused on individual visibility.

⸻

Report

A structured presentation of historical or current execution information.

⸻

CRS Concepts

Commitment Reliability Score (CRS)

A metric designed to measure how consistently a person fulfills commitments over time.

⸻

Reliability

The ability to consistently complete committed work.

⸻

Execution Consistency

The degree to which commitments are fulfilled across multiple planning cycles.

⸻

Artificial Intelligence Concepts

AI Summary

An automatically generated summary of execution activity.

⸻

AI Insight

A recommendation, trend, or observation generated using platform data.

⸻

Execution Intelligence

The combination of commitments, outcomes, CRS metrics, and AI-generated analysis used to improve decision-making.

⸻

Strategic Concepts

Accountability

Visibility into commitments and results.

⸻

Commitment Reliability Management

The discipline of measuring, monitoring, and improving commitment fulfillment and execution consistency.

This represents the strategic category created by Priorities Tracker.

⸻

Ubiquitous Language

The following terms should be used consistently throughout all project documentation:

* Commitment
* Priority
* Task
* Planning Cycle
* Check-In
* Check-Out
* Blocker
* Risk
* CRS
* Reliability
* Team
* Project
* Phase
* Dashboard
* AI Summary
* AI Insight

These terms constitute the official ubiquitous language of Priorities Tracker.
