AI Product Strategy

Executive Summary

Artificial Intelligence is not the core product of Priorities Tracker.

The core product is the weekly commitment management process and the Commitment Reliability Score (CRS).

AI serves as an amplification layer that helps managers and organizations extract more value from the information already captured by the platform.

The role of AI is to transform weekly planning data, execution results, risks, blockers, and reliability metrics into actionable insights that improve decision-making and reduce management overhead.

The strategy is intentionally conservative.

Priorities Tracker should never depend on AI to operate.

Instead, AI should reduce friction, improve visibility, and help managers identify patterns that would otherwise require manual analysis.

⸻

Strategic Principles

The AI strategy is guided by five principles.

AI as an Amplifier

Artificial Intelligence should enhance human decision-making.

It should not replace managers, employees, or organizational processes.

The platform’s value must remain intact even when AI capabilities are disabled.

⸻

AI as a Time Saver

Managers spend significant time reviewing updates, preparing for meetings, and identifying risks.

AI should reduce this effort by automatically summarizing information and highlighting relevant patterns.

⸻

AI as an Insight Engine

AI should identify relationships and trends that are difficult to detect manually.

Examples include:

* Recurring blockers
* Repeated carry-over priorities
* Declining CRS trends
* Risk concentration within projects

⸻

AI as a Recommendation Layer

AI should provide suggestions and observations.

Final decisions remain with managers and organizational leaders.

⸻

AI Must Be Explainable

Managers should understand why an insight or recommendation was generated.

Opaque or unexplainable conclusions reduce trust and adoption.

⸻

Product Objectives

The AI strategy exists to support several product objectives.

Objective 1: Reduce Management Overhead

Minimize time spent collecting and interpreting status information.

⸻

Objective 2: Improve Visibility

Help managers quickly understand team conditions.

⸻

Objective 3: Improve Coaching Conversations

Provide relevant context for one-on-one meetings.

⸻

Objective 4: Improve Risk Detection

Identify execution issues before they become significant problems.

⸻

Objective 5: Increase Organizational Intelligence

Transform operational data into actionable knowledge.

⸻

MVP AI Capability

The MVP includes a single AI feature.

Weekly Summary Generation

Purpose

Provide managers with an automatically generated executive summary of team activity.

Inputs

The summary is generated using:

* Check-In data
* Check-Out data
* Priorities
* Tasks
* Risks
* Blockers
* CRS

Example

This week the team completed 87% of its commitments. Three priorities are currently at risk and two recurring blockers were identified. Overall CRS remained stable compared to the previous week.

Benefits

* Faster weekly reviews.
* Reduced manual reporting.
* Improved manager visibility.

⸻

Phase 2 AI Capabilities

Once the MVP is validated, additional capabilities can be introduced.

⸻

One-on-One Preparation Assistant

Purpose

Help managers prepare for employee conversations.

Generated Information

* Completed priorities.
* Pending priorities.
* Active blockers.
* Open risks.
* CRS evolution.
* Significant changes since previous weeks.

Example Questions

The assistant may help managers answer:

* What changed since the last meeting?
* Which commitments remain unfinished?
* Is reliability improving or declining?

⸻

Employee Progress Summaries

Generate concise summaries of individual execution history.

Example

During the last eight weeks, the employee maintained an average CRS of 91 and completed 94% of committed priorities.

⸻

Phase 3 AI Capabilities

These capabilities introduce deeper organizational intelligence.

⸻

Manager Insights

AI identifies patterns that may not be immediately visible.

Examples

Juan has carried over priorities during four consecutive weeks.

Ana has maintained a CRS above 90 for eight consecutive weeks.

The CRM project contains 60% of all active blockers.

⸻

Trend Analysis

Detect long-term changes in execution behavior.

Examples

* Consistent CRS improvement.
* Reliability deterioration.
* Increased blocker frequency.
* Reduced completion rates.

⸻

Risk Detection

Identify emerging execution risks before they impact delivery.

Potential indicators include:

* Increasing carry-over rates.
* Declining completion percentages.
* Rising blocker counts.
* Sudden CRS changes.

⸻

Long-Term AI Vision

The long-term objective is to create an organizational intelligence layer that helps leaders understand execution health across teams.

AI should eventually provide answers to questions such as:

* Which teams are most reliable?
* Which projects present the highest risk?
* Where are blockers accumulating?
* Which employees may benefit from coaching?
* Which organizational patterns impact execution?

⸻

AI and CRS

AI and CRS are complementary capabilities.

CRS Provides Measurement

CRS quantifies execution reliability.

It answers:

How reliable is this person?

⸻

AI Provides Interpretation

AI explains what may be causing changes in reliability.

It answers:

Why is reliability improving or declining?

Together they create a more complete understanding of execution performance.

⸻

Risks

Risk 1: Over-Reliance on AI

Managers may trust generated insights without validating context.

Mitigation

Present AI output as recommendations rather than facts.

⸻

Risk 2: Low-Quality Inputs

AI quality depends on the quality of user-provided information.

Mitigation

Encourage simple but consistent weekly updates.

⸻

Risk 3: Excessive Complexity

Too many AI features can distract from the product’s primary purpose.

Mitigation

Maintain focus on commitment management and execution visibility.

⸻

Strategic Positioning

Many products add AI as a feature.

Priorities Tracker uses AI to strengthen its core mission:

Improve commitment visibility, execution reliability, and managerial effectiveness.

AI is not the product.

AI makes the product more useful.

⸻

Strategic Conclusion

Artificial Intelligence should serve as an intelligence layer built on top of the weekly planning process and CRS.

Its purpose is to reduce effort, improve visibility, and help managers make better decisions.

The long-term vision is not to automate management.

The long-term vision is to make managers better informed, better prepared, and more effective through actionable execution intelligence.
